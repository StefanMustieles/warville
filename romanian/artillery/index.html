1.3cm Hotchkiss Anti-Aircraft Machine-Gun Model 1931

  • Year: August 1937.
  • Numbers Produced: 18.702.
  • Calibre: 37mm (37x223).
  • Overall Length: 3.92m.
  • Barrel Length: 2.1m (L/57).
  • Carriage: Split Trail.
  • Gun Shield: 6.35mm.
  • Breech: Semi-Automatic/Vertical Block.
  • Recoil: Hydrospring.
  • Weight in Action: 413.68 kg.
  • Gun Sight: M40.
  • Magnification: 1.44x
  • Elevation: -10° to +15°.
  • Traverse: 60°.
  • Shell Weights/Types:
    APC (M51) 1.579 kg.
    HE (M63) 1.420 kg.
    Canister (M2) 1.583 kg.
  • Muzzle Velocity:
    APC (M51B) 554 m/s.
    HE (M63) 792 m/s.
    Canister (M2) 762 m/s.
  • Rate of Fire: 25 r.p.m.
  • Penetration: 58mm @ 500m @ 30° / 50mm @ 1000m @ 30°.
  • Maximum APC Range: 6.858m.
  • Maximum HE Range: 11.704m.
  • Maximum Canister Range: 500m.
  • Crew (Total 6):
    Corporal/Squad Leader + .30 M1 Carbine.
    Pvt/Gunner + .30 M1 Carbine.
    Pvt/Assistant Gunner + .30 M1 Carbine.
    2 x Pvts/Ammo Bearers + .30 M1 Carbines.
    2 x Pvts/Drivers + .30 M1 Rifles (Towing Vehicles).
  • Traction: Motorised (Willys Jeep & Dodge Trucks.
  • Notes: Adopted by the American Army in 1940, the M3 became the standard Anti-Tank Gun of the U.S. Ground Forces in the early part of WW2, but later replaced by the 57mm M1. Due to its light weight it was kept in use in the Pacific theatre as an Infantry Gun, and used to fire canister shot to devastating effect on Japanese Infantry attacks
