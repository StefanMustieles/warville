20 ltK/35

(SA-kuva.fi)

  • Year: 1942 (M51) 1944 (M55).
  • Total: ? (M51) 10.000 (M55).
  • Calibre: .50 Cal (12.7x99 M2 U.S. Ball).
  • Barrel Length: 1.14m.
  • Length of Weapon: 1.95m. (1.65m)
  • Width: 2.10m.
  • Height: 1.43m.
  • Gun Mounts: Maxson M45C (M51) Maxson M45F (M55).
  • Trailers; M17 (M51) M20 (M55).
  • Length (M51): 4.877m.
  • Width: 2.438m.
  • Height: 1.512m.
  • Length (M55): 1.651m.
  • Width: 2.092m.
  • Height: 1.607m.
  • Elevation: -10° to +90°.
  • Traverse: 360°.
  • Gun Sight: M18.
  • Round Weight: 117.4 g.
  • Muzzle Velocity: 856 m/s.
  • Armoured Plate: 6.4mm.
  • Weight;
  • M51: 3.128 kg
  • M55: 1.338 kg.
  • Magazine Capacity: 800 x .50 (in four 200 x Round Drum Magazines).
  • Maximum Ceiling: 4.570m.
  • Maximum Ground Range: 6.770m.
  • Maximum Rate of Fire: 2.200 rpm.
  • Armour Penetration; 12mm @ 500m @ 30°.
  • Crew (Total 5):
    • Sergeant/Squad Leader.
    Corporal/Gunner.
    Two Pvts/Loaders.
    Pvt/Driver.
  • Traction: 2 ½ ton Truck (M51) 3/4 Truck (M55).
  • Notes: The M51 was composed of the M17 trailer and the M45 multiple .50 cal. Mount. The M55 was the combination of the M45 mounted on the lighter M20 Trailer. Quad .50s were usually kept under AAA Group command and issued at Divisional level.
