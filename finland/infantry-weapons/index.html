M/23 Pistol

  • Year: March 1911.
  • Numbers Produced: 2.300.000.
  • Calibre: .45 APC (11.43x23).
  • Cartridge Weight: 19.01g.
  • Barrel Length: 127mm.
  • Overall Length: 210mm.
  • Combat Weight: 1.36 kg.
  • Operation: Single Action Semi-Automatic.
  • Sights: Front Blade & Rear Notched.
  • Feed: 7 Round Box Magazine.
  • Rate of Fire: 21 r.p.m.
  • Muzzle Velocity: 253 m/s.
  • Effective Range: 25m.
  • Maximum Range: 50m.
  • Notes: When the US Military adopted a new Pistol, they went for one with stopping power, they decided on the Browning .45 M1911 and it became the standard US service pistol in four major conflicts and remained in service until 1984.

Suomi M31

(Finnish Defence Forces)

  • Year: January 1942.
  • Numbers Produced: 100.000.
  • Calibre: .45 ACP (11.43x23).
  • Cartridge Weight: 19.01g.
  • Barrel Length: 279mm.
  • Overall Length: 959mm.
  • Combat Weight: 3.06 kg.
  • Operation: Delayed Blowback.
  • Sights: Front Blade & Rear Notch.
  • Feed: 20 Round Box.
  • Practical Rate of Fire: 200 r.p.m.
  • Maximum Rate of Fire: 550 r.p.m.
  • Muzzle Velocity: 280 m/s.
  • Effective Range: 50m.
  • Maximum Range: 150m.
  • Notes: Adopted mainly by the US Marine Corps due to the lack of Thompsons, and was withdrawn from combat in 1943 when the M3 S.M.G. became available.

M/27 Rifle

  • Year: December 1942 M3) December 1944 (M3A1).
  • Numbers Produced: 680.000 (M3) 15.469 (M3A1).
  • Calibre: .45 ACP (11.43x23).
  • Cartridge Weight: 19.01g.
  • Barrel Length: 205mm.
  • Overall Length: 750mm.
  • Combat Weight: 4.52 kg (M3) 4.43 kg (M3A1).
  • Operation: BlowBack / Fully Automatic.
  • Sights: Front Blade and Fixed Rear Peep.
  • Feed: 30 Round Box Magazine.
  • Practical Rate of Fire: 120 r.p.m.
  • Maximum Rate of Fire: 450 r.p.m.
  • Muzzle Velocity: 280 m/s.
  • Effective Range: 50m.
  • Maximum Range: 150m.
  • Notes: After the British introduced the Sten Sub-Machine gun in 1941, the US decided to adopt a similar design by using a pressed metal construction to make a cheap and simple weapon, the new gun was called the M3 and was first introduced in late 1942, with a the modified M3A1 arriving late 1944. After WW2 the M3 was mainly issued to Tank Crews.

M/39 Rifle

  • Year: January 1940 (M1928A1) April 1942 (M1) October 1942 (M1A1).
  • Numbers Produced: 562.511 (M1928A1) 285.480 (M1) 539.143 (M1A1).
  • Calibre: .45 APC (11.43x23)..
  • Cartridge Weight: 19.01g.
  • Barrel Length: 260mm.
  • Overall Length: 810mm.
  • Combat Weight: 5.4 kg (M1928) 5.31 kg (M1 & M1A1).
  • Operation: Blow Back Fully Automatic.
  • Sights: Front Blade and Fixed Rear Peep.
  • Feed: 30 Round Box or 50 Round Drum Magazine (M1928A1 only).
  • Practical Rate of Fire: 210 r.p.m.
  • Maximum Rate of Fire: 700 r.p.m.
  • Muzzle Velocity: 280 m/s.
  • Effective Range: 50m.
  • Maximum Range: 150m.
  • Notes: The USA entered the war with the M1928A1 as its primary S.M.G. this was very expensive to produce so a simpler version (the M1) was developed, this could not use the drum magazine issued to the M1928A1, but is was more reliable. The M1 became the most common version in WW2. The M1A1 was a simplified version of the M1 and saw service in Korea.
